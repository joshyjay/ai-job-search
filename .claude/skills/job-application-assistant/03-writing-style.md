# Writing Style Guide

This supersedes the stock guide. It encodes a tested voice. Do not soften it
toward generic cover-letter advice. In particular, cover letters are PROSE, not
bullets, and never carry subheadings.

## Hard voice rules (apply to CV and cover letter)

1. No colons in body text or headings.
2. No em dashes, and no hyphens used as separators. Use commas or restructure.
3. No tricolons or serial lists in prose. Maximum two items joined by "and".
   The only permitted three-part constructions are the country and discipline
   career arc (Ghana, Egypt, Denmark) and the standard close
   (location, availability, openness).
4. No "but" pivots, no negative to positive structures ("not X, it is Y",
   "although X, I").
5. No self-promotional summary lines ("I would bring a combination of",
   "my background fits well", "strong fit for"). Let the work state the fit.
6. No punchy fragments planted for drama.
7. No corporate filler or LLM openers. Cut "I am passionate about",
   "leverage", "hit the ground running", "drive results", "synergies".
8. Honesty as a confidence move, not a hedge. "The model itself was the
   smallest part of it" reads as command of the work and redirects to the
   transferable part. Use it that way.
9. Every claim survives an interview. Name gaps live at interview, never
   pre-justify them on the page.

## Overclaiming (hard line)

Reframe emphasis, not substance. Apply the interview backtrack test: if the
candidate would have to say "well, what I actually meant was", it is too far.
- OK: reordering to lead with the most relevant work, natural domain synonyms,
  emphasising one aspect of a broad role.
- Never: claiming experience not held, implying industry work that was
  academic, naming a tool or platform not actually used (e.g. do not claim
  Vertex AI or Azure AI; OpenAI via Codex is real and may be named).
Established precedents that stay enforced: CFD/ANSYS are minor rusty MSc work,
not selling points. FastAPI/PostgreSQL ownership claims that were a
collaborator's work stay out. No fabricated bullets (IMO officials, external
client presentations). Check the master profile and prior files rather than
inventing details.

## Cover letter (Hook, Proof, Range, Close)

Four paragraphs, prose, one page. No bullets. No subheadings. See
`06-cover-letter-templates.md` and `cover_letters/cover_template.tex` for the
worked exemplar.

1. HOOK. Open on a concrete thing that actually happened, chosen because it
   maps onto the role's core problem. Never "I am applying for", never an
   abstraction about the role.
2. PROOF. Speak to the role's central function in plain prose. Name the actual
   work. Show the all-in-one value, that the candidate runs a thing and builds,
   documents, and measures it.
3. RANGE plus MOTIVATION. State the multi-country, multi-discipline path as
   range, never as apology for the degree. Then why this employer, honest and
   specific to their actual situation, not a generic mission line.
4. CLOSE. Location, availability, openness. Short. Currently in the Copenhagen
   area, available to start now, ready to relocate immediately for the right
   role.

Match the language of the posting. Address a named person if the posting gives
one, otherwise "Dear Hiring Team". Any mention of AI tooling names the tools
actually used (Claude Code, Codex, GitHub Copilot).

## CV style

- All text black. Hyperlinks navy, behind words, never bare URLs.
- Concise verb-led single-line bullets. Action, then method, then result, then
  relevance to the target role. Ownership verbs.
- Lead with adoption and ownership signals. The strongest is that the camera
  systems became the standard imaging infrastructure at CoaST, replacing manual
  inspection. Signals that work was trusted, adopted, and ran reliably beat
  descriptions of what a system does.
- Skills as single `\mbox` lines per category so they survive ATS parsing.
- Profile must not duplicate facts from the experience section.
- Publications grouped peer reviewed, then submitted and in preparation.
- No phone number. No gaps section (handled live at interview).
- English listed as Native, never Fluent. Danish as Actively learning.
- ML and image processing stay visible on every CV regardless of the lead.
- Education dates (canonical): PhD DTU 2023 to 2026, MSc EJUST 2020 to 2023,
  BSc KNUST 2014 to 2018.
- Two pages for substantive roles, one page for junior or software developer
  roles. Education leads experience where the degree is the primary filter.

## Tone

Warm but direct. Confident without cockiness, which is what Danish hiring
rewards. First person, active voice. Demonstrate, do not state.
