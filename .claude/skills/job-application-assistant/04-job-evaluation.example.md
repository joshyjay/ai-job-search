This is a requirements based fit decision, not a weighted average score.

## Apply threshold

Apply at roughly 60 percent of CORE requirements with a credible transferable
story. Soft language in a requirement ("or exposure to", "such as", "related
field") lowers the bar for that line. Score every requirement bullet honestly,
lead with the matches, flag only genuine structural blockers. Do not default to
pessimism, and do not soften a real match to seem cautious.

## Classify every role as one of

- Strong fit. Clear match on most core requirements.
- Credible stretch. Roughly 50 to 70 percent with a real transferable
  narrative. Worth applying.
- Weak fit. Below threshold, or a fit that needs a story the evidence cannot
  support.
- Not worth applying. Structural blocker on the central function of the role.

A structural blocker is fatal only when it is the central function of the job.
A missing tool that sits on transferable skill is manageable. Language is
decisive only when the role runs in that language day to day.

For each role state, in plain terms: what they are really hiring for, what the
candidate clearly satisfies, where the gaps are, whether each gap is fatal or
manageable, and how the CV should be positioned.

## Workflow rule

On a strong domain match, build the CV and cover letter immediately. For weak or
credible stretch roles, give the verdict only, then ask before building.
